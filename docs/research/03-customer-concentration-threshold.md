## M&A Practitioner Findings on Customer Concentration Risk

M&A practitioner literature and lending guidelines consistently identify specific thresholds for customer concentration risk, with **20–25%** revenue from a single client serving as the primary benchmark for applying a valuation discount. Severe risk is documented at the **50%** threshold, which often triggers structural deal changes (earnouts) or a reduction in the EBITDA/SDE multiple of **0.5x to 1.0x turns**.

## 1. The 25% Recognition Threshold

The **20–25%** range is a standard threshold in SME M&A literature for "customer concentration risk." Above this level, the dependency is no longer considered a "growth engine" but a "structural vulnerability".

- **Source Authority:** **BDO Transaction Advisory Group** and **Exit Strategies Group**.
- **Documented Finding:** "As a general rule, no single customer should account for more than 20-25% of your company's revenue... it represents substantial risk to you and the next owner".
- **SaaS/Tech Benchmarks:** For recurring revenue models, the threshold is even lower; risk is flagged if any single customer exceeds **10%** of ARR, or if the top 5–10 customers account for more than **40–50%**.

## 2. The 50% "Severe Risk" Threshold

A **50%** concentration in one customer or a small group (top three) is widely recognized as "severe" or "extreme" risk, often rendering a business "unsalable" without massive contingent structures.

- **Source Authority:** **Walden M&A** and **Morgan & Westfield**.
- **Exact Finding:** "If 50% of the revenue depends on a single client, it introduces massive risk for any buyer... if you have customer concentration above 30% to 50%, then your business may be unsalable unless you are willing to agree to a deal structure that offers the buyer numerous protections".
- **Lending Impact:** Lenders typically tighten Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) requirements to **1.50x** (up from the 1.25x standard) for businesses with 50% concentration to buffer against default.

## 3. Quantified Multiple Discounts (Absolute Terms)

Practitioner literature quantifies the discount for concentration in absolute multiple "turns." A typical reduction ranges from **-0.5x to -1.0x points** off the baseline EBITDA or SDE multiple.

| Concentration Level | Multiple Reduction | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Single Customer > 20% | -0.5x to -1.0x | DealStream (S_S172) |
| Single Customer > 30% | -1.0x | DealStream (S_S60) |
| Single Customer > 15% (Handshake relationship) | -0.5x to -1.0x | DealFax (S_S105/S_S182) |

- **Source Passage:** "If one client represents 30% of revenue, subtract 0.5x from the revenue multiple or 1x from the EBITDA multiple".
- **Value Reduction %:** This turn compression typically correlates to a **10–30%** total enterprise value reduction.

## 4. IBBA, BizBuySell, and SBA References

Industry reports and lending data from 2023–2025 confirm these thresholds as active underwriting and pricing criteria.

- **IBBA Market Pulse (Q4 2023):** Identifies revenue tied to owner-presence and customer concentration as the "most cited factors in deal restructuring and earnout conditions".
- **BizBuySell (Q4 2024 Market Report):** States that businesses where no single customer exceeds **10%** of revenue sell **47% faster** and at **18% higher multiples** because they avoid the risk discount.
- **SBA Lending (Live Oak Bank):** Data indicates that diversifying a customer base below 10% per client reduces the risk discount applied by lenders and buyers by **20–30%**.
- **SBA SOP 50 10 8:** While the SOP does not mandate a specific concentration %, it requires lenders to document "adequacy of collateral" and "identifiable weakness in the credit," with conservative lenders using a **1.50x DSCR floor** for concentrated businesses.
